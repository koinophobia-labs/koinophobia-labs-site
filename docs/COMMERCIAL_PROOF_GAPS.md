# Koinophobia Labs Commercial Proof Gaps

Last reviewed: July 17, 2026

This document records the evidence boundary used by the public website. It should be updated whenever a project changes status or new client permission is received.

## Proof currently available

- Career Forge: internal product with a public working link and public repository link.
- Trendi: internal product with a product route, working demo media, and interface screenshots.
- You Know Ball: internal product with a live web MVP, product screenshots, documented guardrails, and existing release checks.
- Blackline Ritual: clearly labeled concept build for a private tattoo-studio inquiry path.
- Iron Method Coaching: clearly labeled concept build for a local coaching and trial-inquiry path.
- Forge & Foam: clearly labeled concept build for a neighborhood cafe website.
- A documented six-step delivery process covering diagnosis, scope, build, review, launch, and support.
- Published price ranges, typical timelines, ownership expectations, revision language, and support language.
- A working Revenue Leak Audit offer, intake path, audit-generation system, proposal workflow, payment foundation, and CRM routes in the repository.
- Existing responsive product visuals, deployment configuration, automated tests, and release-check scripts.

## Proof still needed

- Completed external client projects with permission to publish.
- Verified client-pilot approvals and accurate current status.
- Testimonials supplied by real clients and approved for publication.
- Permission to publish client names, logos, screenshots, and business context.
- Measured before-and-after business results with a documented measurement method.
- Public live client links.
- Verified promised timelines and actual delivery dates.
- Client references willing to speak with prospective buyers.
- Support outcomes after the defined post-launch window.
- Evidence supporting any future claim that a concept has become a pilot or live client engagement.

## Recommended evidence to collect from every future client

- Original business problem in the client's words.
- Starting screenshots of the website, workflow, forms, and relevant tools.
- Approved scope, price, responsibilities, revision rounds, and success criteria.
- Promised start and delivery dates.
- Actual start and delivery dates, including any documented schedule changes.
- Final desktop and mobile screenshots.
- A workflow diagram or plain-language customer journey.
- Measurable result, measurement window, data source, and baseline.
- A client quote in the client's own words.
- The client's approval of the final quote and attribution.
- Written permission to use the business name, logo, screenshots, and link.
- Live project URL and launch date.
- Post-launch support issues and resolution outcome.

## Publishing rules

1. Keep concept builds labeled as concepts until written evidence supports another status.
2. Do not promote a proposal or conversation to Client Pilot without external approval.
3. Do not promote a pilot to Live Client until delivery status is verifiable.
4. Keep intended impact separate from measured results.
5. Omit testimonial, metric, timeline, image, or external-link fields when the evidence is unavailable.
6. Add new projects through `lib/commercial.ts` so the work index and case-study system share one source of truth.
